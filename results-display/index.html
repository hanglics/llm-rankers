Step 1 / 4 stable baseline

    Current Evidence

    What the data supports, what it rejects, and why it still matters

    Supported

    DualEnd is the strongest family overall

    DualEnd is positive in 14/18 TREC DL configurations and wins all 12/12 Qwen configurations, even though the corrected significance is still limited on these small query sets.

    Rejected

    Worst-only is not a mirror of best-only

    BottomUp loses in all 18 configurations and has 6 Bonferroni-significant losses. The worst prompt is not a simple symmetric inverse of the best prompt.

    Why it matters

    The interesting signal is joint elicitation

    The key novelty is behavioral: asking for best and worst together changes the model's bias pattern and can add a modest refinement signal that does not survive when worst-selection is isolated.

    DualEnd positive deltas 14 / 18
    BottomUp Bonferroni-significant losses 6 / 18
    BiDir Bonferroni-significant losses 3 / 18
    Behavioral Fingerprint

    The current mechanism story in one screen

    Agreement

    DualEnd stays close to TopDown

    Overlap@10 = 7.01 and Kendall tau = 0.9254 between TopDown and DualEnd. That is much closer than TopDown vs BottomUp.

    Bias

    dual_worst flips the positional pattern

    Standalone worst-selection leans hard toward the last passage, but dual_worst shifts toward primacy instead: A ≈ 0.35, D ≈ 0.14.

    Interpretation

    Worst helps only when it shares the same prompt

    Independent BottomUp hurts. Independent fusion hurts. Same-call best-plus-worst is the only version that looks meaningfully useful.
